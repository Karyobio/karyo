Our Approach

Developing a framework around scientific discovery

Problems can be divided into two categories, systemic & scientific. Systemic problems are those that can be solved through a change in process. So although it might be difficult to pinpoint a root cause, solutions often will require the assembly of existing knowledge (ie software to automate a process).

Science is different. To solve a scientific problem is to discover a new process. The only common framework for doing so is the scientific method. As we work on developing novel therapies, we're attempting to improve on the scientific method by developing a new relaible framework that can help standardize discovery.

Projects

01

EVacc

RNA viruses possess a high affinity towards mutation through antigenic drift & shift. This leads to both seasonal disease (Influenza) & Pandemics (Covid-19). Building off natural methods of antibody diversification in B-Cell's, Karyo is developing a therapy framework to tackle rapidly mutating diseases with an endogenous evoliving vaccine (EVacc) produced directly by the cells of a host.

02

SELECT

A positive correlation exists between the presence of T memory stem cells (TSCM's) and the response to CAR T Cell therapy. At Karyo, we've devised SELECT (Surface-Engineered Lentiviral based Transduction of CAR on TSCM's), a novel, in-vivo theraputic approach which specifically targets highly proliferating TSCM's. This approach simultaneously lowers the cost and raises the efficacy of CAR T Cell therapy.

03

Immune 2.0

The world is more connected than ever. While generally helpful, this increases the tail risk of a rapidly-spreading global pandemic (as seen with COVID-19). At Karyo, we're leveraging machine learning & computational biology to predict the binding of T-cells to antigens, which will allows us to predict the efficacy of current immunotherapies as well as quickly develop new epitope vaccine candidates.
